About Us


FortifyKenya is a comprehensive project dedicated to analyzing the rapidly increasing number of cyber attacks reported in Kenya, with a particular focus on the financial sector. As cyber threats become more prevalent and sophisticated, FortifyKenya aims to understand the evolving cyber threat landscape by identifying the tactics and techniques used by threat actors. The project also pinpoints the most targeted areas within Kenya, offering invaluable insights into regional vulnerabilities. By doing so, FortifyKenya provides some of the best practices to prevent cyber attacks, ensuring the financial sector is equipped with the necessary tools and knowledge to safeguard against potential threats. In-depth Analysis

At Fortifykenya we are dedicated to meticulously analyzing the cybersecurity threat landscape in Kenya, delivering accurate and reliable information to protect the nation's digital ecosystem. Banking and Financial Services

Kenya's banking sector faces significant cyber threats, such as the attacks on KCB and National Bank. Phishing, malware, ransomware, insider threats, and DDoS attacks are prevalent. Effective countermeasures include multi-factor authentication, cybersecurity training, advanced threat detection, and strong access controls to protect systems and customer data.

Click to see more           

Capital Markets and Securities

Kenya's capital markets face significant cyber threats, such as the 2016 CDSC data breach and the 2019 NSE attack, which disrupted trading. Risks include data breaches, trading disruptions, and market manipulation. Mitigation strategies involve robust encryption, secure communication channels, intrusion detection systems, regular security assessments, and collaboration with regulators for shared threat intelligence.

Financial Technology (FinTech) and Payment Systems

The rapidly growing fintech industry faces significant cyber risks. The widespread adoption of fintech solutions increases these threats, while regulatory compliance adds complexity. Cyber incidents can be financially devastating, with average data breaches costing $5.9 million and major cyberattacks potentially costing the global economy $3.5 trillion.
