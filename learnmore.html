Cyber Threat Landscape Analysis

In the digital age, Kenya finds itself at a crossroads where the promise of technological advancement intertwines with the perils of escalating cyber threats. Cyber incidents have become rampant, permeating every facet of our society, from individual users to large corporations and vital government institutions.Kenya has recorded a significant increase in cyber threats, escalating from 7.7 million in 2016 to an alarming 444 million in 2022. According to the latest report published on April 4, 2024 by Communications Authority of Kenya, Kenya has detected over 900 million cyber threats, highlighting an exponential growth that is cause for serious concern. This upward trajectory underscores the urgent need for enhanced cybersecurity measures and robust strategies to combat the rising threat landscape, ensuring the safety and security of the nation's digital infrastructure.


Kenya's Top Cyber Threat Zones


The following are major areas in Kenya that are significantly affected by cyber threats. Many of these areas are frequently misunderstood, leading to rampant cyber attacks.

          
NAIROBI

Nairobi, being the capital and business hub, sees many phishing attacks targeting both individuals and businesses, particularly in the banking and financial sectors. Numerous businesses in Nairobi have reported financial losses due to BEC scams

          
MOMBASA

In Mombasa, The maritime and shipping sectors have reported incidents of cyber espionage, where sensitive information was stolen. The hospitality industry, including hotels and resorts, has faced ransomware attacks, affecting their operations

          
KIAMBU

In Kiambu county, most Schools and manufacturing sectors have experienced ransomware attacks, causing disruptions.!Educational institutions and manufacturing companies in Thika are often targeted by phishing schemes

          
KISUMU

In Kisumu, Local government offices have been targeted through social engineering tactics. Financial institutions and local businesses in Kisumu have been targeted by phishing attacks.


Recent Cybersecurity Incidents
  • Kenya Airways Ltd.: Suffered a major ransomware attack on Dec. 30, 2023, which led to the leak of confidential company information on the web.
  • Education Sector: Numerous students have fallen victim to scams involving fake scholarship advertisements disseminated through major communication platforms like WhatsApp, Instagram, and Facebook.
  • Social Media Account Hacking: A significant number of Kenyans have been targeted, with high-profile incidents like the hacking of Citizen TV’s YouTube channel highlighting vulnerabilities on platforms such as Facebook and X (formerly Twitter).
  • Kabarak University: Recently experienced a Facebook account hack, causing significant disruption to its online presence and communication channels.
  • e-Citizen Platform: Encountered a DDoS attack in July 2023, leading to temporary unavailability. The government confirmed that no data was lost.

Major Threats In Kenya

Rising Cyber Threats

There has been a significant increase in cyber threats with billions of attacks being detected. This indicates a growing vulnerability of Kenya's digital infrastructure to malicious actors thus calling for action in preventing attacks.

Sophisticated Attacks

Cybercriminals are employing advanced tactics to breach systems and networks by leveraging AI functionalities. This indicates a need for robust cybersecurity measures to predict and subsequently counter these attacks in a timely manner.

Exploitation Of System Vulnerabilities

Many of the detected attacks exploit vulnerabilities in systems, which may be attributed to the insecurity of IoT devices. This underscores the importance of addressing vulnerabilities in various devices

Limited CyberSecurity Skills

There is an acute shortage of skilled cybersecurity professionals in the country which directly hinders effective response to threats. This calls for a robust campaign in enhancing awareness and the importance of such skillsets in the tech industry.

Increasing Cybercrime Incidents

Thousands of cybercrime investigations are currently being facilitated by the Digital Forensics Lab indicating an increase in incidents occuring in Kenya. This informs the importance of spearheading initiatives to curb such attacks.
